Evan Ross Biography

Evan Ross was born in Greenwich, Connecticut, the United States of America, on August 26, 1988. Evan Olav Ross-Naess is his full name. Evan is currently 35 years old and a citizen of the United States. Furthermore, this actor was born under the sign of Virgo and is of German-All-American descent. He was born to Diana Ross (mother) and Arne Naess Jr. (father).

His father, Arne, was a mountaineer and a businessman. Diana, his mother, was an actress. They divorced in the year 2000. Mr. Arne Naess Jr. was tragically killed in a car accident in Cape Town in 2004. His mother remarried and has two daughters, Tracee and Chudney, with Robert Ellis Silberstein. In his father’s second and third marriages, he had Christoffer, Katinka, Leona, Nicklas, and Louis.

Career

Evan Ross began his acting career in the American comedy-drama film ‘ATL,’ starring rappers T.I. and Big Boi, in March 2006, while still a student at Greenwich High School. Prior to making his feature film debut, he appeared in the music video for The Notorious B.I.G.’s song “Nasty Girl.” In 2007, he starred in the HBO television film ‘Life Support,’ alongside Queen Latifah, and received positive feedback for his portrayal of troubled adolescent Amare McCarter. In the film, Tracee Ellis Ross, his real-life half-sister, plays his elder sister.

In the 2007 biographical film ‘Pride,’ he played a good-natured youngster with a speech impediment. The following year, he appeared in the episode ‘What’s Black-A-Lackin’?’ of his sister Tracee’s The CW television series ‘Girlfriends.’ In 2008, he co-starred with Cuba Gooding, Jr. in the thriller ‘Linewatch,’ and he had a major role in the drama film ‘Gardens of the Night,’ also in 2008.

In 2009, he co-starred with Hilary Duff in the crime drama film ‘Life Is Hot in Cracktown,’ and he played Romeo in the comedy-drama film ‘According to Greta.’ He then appears in Lionel Richie’s “Just Go” music video. In 2010, he starred in the drama film ‘Case 219,’ and in the third season of The CW’s teen drama series ‘90210,’ he played a recurring character. That same year, he co-starred with Danny Glover and Nia Long in the critically acclaimed independent film ‘Mooz-lum,’ which depicted how the September 11 attacks affected an African American Muslim family.

He won the ‘Breakthrough Performance award at the SXSW Film Festival for his role in the thriller ’96 Minutes,’ which also starred Brittany Snow. Among his other film and television credits, that year were the comedy-drama film ‘The Family Tree’ and the pilot episode of the television series Luck.’

He only appeared in one film in 2012, the comedy-drama ‘Jeff, Who Lives at Home,’ which starred Jason Segel and Ed Helms. The following year, he was cast as music producer Dallas Austin in the VH1 historical telefilm ‘CrazySexyCool: The TLC Story,’ which was based on the 1990s Atlanta girl group TLC.

In 2014, he appeared in several films, including the drama ‘All the Wilderness,’ the black comedy ‘Just Before I Go,’ and the horror Supremacy.’ The first received favorable reviews from critics, while the second and third disappointed both critics and audiences.
