Professional Life

Howard Stern began his professional career as a marketing executive at “Benton & Bowles,” a New York advertising agency, where he worked for several years. Following that, he met with the agency’s creative staff to discuss their ideas. Given that Stern was certain that he wanted to work in a radio station, he began looking for opportunities in the entertainment industry. A radio sales position opened up for him, which finally led to his working the midday shift at a local radio station for four hours. Stern was promoted to the position of production director at the station.

In addition to his producing job, Stern began appearing in commercials and stand-up comedy, which helped him earn some notoriety. His first independent show was produced in collaboration with the WWWW radio station. Despite the fact that the station was not progressing, Stern’s work was becoming more and more popular. Unfortunately, WWWW did not achieve widespread acceptance, and as a result, he was forced to resign from his position. The next year, Stern was hired to host the morning shows for WWDC, a radio station based in Washington, DC. It was a smash hit, and Stern received an offer from WNBC for a five-year contract.

As a result of his signature on the contract, the host was invited to co-host an afternoon session with WNBC. In August 1982, he began his association with WNBC and made his first appearance on the television talk show “Late Night with David Letterman.” The host was also featured in the People magazine issue of the same year. But Stern had a falling out with the administration of WNBC, and his contract with the network was terminated in the year 1985 as a result of this. Then Stern began to play live in clubs and other venues around the world.
