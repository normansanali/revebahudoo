Rachel Sennott (born on September 19, 1995) is a 27 years old American comedian and actress famous for her part in the 2020 movie “Shiva Baby.” Sennott is a self-made celebrity because, despite having no connections to the Hollywood industry, she was cast in films based solely on her ability.

As per one of her interviews, she started her career doing comedy gigs at open mics. She appeared in short films and moved on to more significant projects like “Ayo and Rachel are Single” and “Get Money.”

In 2022, this young artist made it into the headlines after landing the role of Alice in Halina Reijn-directed horror-thriller “Bodies Bodies Bodies,” which was released on August 5.

The plot of this film circulates about a group of friends who organize a party at a remote mansion. The party suddenly turns into a deadly game, and the occasion of enjoyment turns into a horror night.

Rachel Sennott Is Raised Catholic

On September 19, 1995, Rachel Anne Sennott (age 27 years) took birth to her father, Jack Sennott, and mother, Donna Sennott, in Simsbury Town of Connecticut. As per sources, Rachel is half Italian and half Irish. Her folks raised her a Catholic. She has a brother named Brian and three sisters, Anna, Lizzie, and Katherine.

Sennott spent her entire childhood in Simsbury and attended Simsbury High School. In 2014, after high school, she joined NYC’s reputed Tisch School of Arts (New York University).

Anne was in the first year of her college when she fell in love with standup comedy and started performing on open mics. Along with humor, she kept giving auditions and landed a role in “Hostess.”

Although Sennott was doing well in acting, she kept doing open mic performances. Even after graduation, she kept doing so. Slowly she also started Twitter comedy and used to post several jokes daily.

How Rachel Sennott Became an Actor?

The professional career of Sennott that started with “Unbalanced” in 2016 kept growing with time. She played several minor roles in TV series like “The Basics,” “Get Money,” and “Stuck in the Mall.” But she faced difficulty making a proper debut in the film industry.

From 2017 to 2019, Rachel worked on several projects, mainly short movies like “Hostess,” “Wakey Wakey,” and “Tabitha in Love,” and when, in 2020, she landed in the role of Hannah Rosen in Olivia Peace-directed film “Tahara,” for which she started gaining media attention.

Her movie career took off when in the same year, Sennott got the chance to also work in the remake of “Shiva Baby,” for which the Bruin Film Society Awards jury gave her the Best Actress award.
