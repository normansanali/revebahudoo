Freddie Freeman | Net Worth and Income

Talented baseball player and athlete Freddie Freeman’s net worth is a whopping $40 million.

In 2007, Freeman signed a rookie contract with the Atlanta Braves, including a $409,500 signing bonus.

The Braves were confident that Freddie was the franchise’s cornerstone after an All-Star year in 2013.

As a result, he agreed to a hefty extension worth $135 million over eight years, including a $2,875,000 payout.

After receiving a big contract, Freeman has proven that he is worth the investment. He made four more All-Star appearances after that.

Similarly, he also won the NL Hank Aaron Award and was named to the All-MLB First Team for the first time in 2020.

Freddie led the Braves to a World Series championship the following year, giving him his first World Series title.

According to sources, each World Series champion earns $400,000 in addition to a championship ring worth $20,000.

Freeman became a free agent after winning a title with the Braves.

Before the end of the transfer window in 2021, he turned down the Braves’ qualifying offer of $125 million over five years.

The Braves reportedly boosted their long-term offer to five years and $140 million during the summer, but Freeman and his agent asked for the sixth year.

His agency issued a request to the Braves, offering them two options and one hour to choose one of them.

The Braves turned down both offers and decided to withdraw their long-standing offer.

Thanks to his spectacular stint with the team, he was one of the top free agents on the market, rating third on the MLB website.

Hence, on March 18, 2022, he accepted a six-year, $162 million agreement with the Los Angeles Dodgers.

Freddie Freeman | Career

The Atlanta Braves selected Freddie in the second round of the 2007 MLB Draft, with the 78th overall selection.

In 2007, Freeman made his career debut with the Gulf Coast Braves. 

Similarly, in 2008, he played for the Class A South Atlantic League’s Rome Braves.

Baseball America classified him as the Braves’ fifth-best prospect before the 2009 season. 

Moreover, Freddie entered the 2011 season as the Braves’ starting first baseman.

His play upgraded after a poor start, and he was acknowledged as a great contender for the newcomer of the year.

Freeman was a finalist in the 2013 Major League Baseball All-Star Game Final Vote in 2013.

He won with a record-breaking 19.7 million fan votes, but he could not play due to a thumb injury sustained three days before the game.

During the first half of the 2019 season, Freddie garnered notice for his offensive production, particularly in June.
