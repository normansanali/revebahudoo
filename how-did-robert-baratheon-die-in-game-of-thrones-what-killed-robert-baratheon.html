How did Robert Baratheon die in Game of Thrones? What killed Robert Baratheon?

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Friday, July 12, 2024

Robert I. Baratheon is the head of House Baratheon in King’s Landing, as well as the King of the Andals, Rhoynar, and First Men, the Lord of the Seven Kingdoms, and the Protector of the Realm of Westeros. During Robert’s Rebellion, King Aerys II Targaryen, Robert’s first cousin once removed, was murdered; as a result, Robert was crowned king.

Following the conflict, Robert married Cersei Lannister to preserve political harmony. Robert and Myrcella had three children: Joffrey, Myrcella, and Tommen. In the television adaptation of Game of Thrones, Mark Addy plays Robert. Black hair and piercing blue eyes give Robert the classic Baratheon look. His chest is covered in dense, coarse black hair around his manhood.

Robert was a handsome young man with clean-shaven skin and tough, strong hands. He was big and strong, with muscles that were “like a maiden’s fantasy.” After putting down Greyjoy’s Rebellion in 289 AC, Robert’s appearance changed. Robert gained a lot of weight as a result of his bingeing and drinking. He has gained at least eight stone in the nine years afterwards.

How did Robert Baratheon die in ‘Game of Thrones’? What killed Robert Baratheon?

Robert is slain by a wild boar in a hunting accident engineered by Cersei, and he names Eddard as regent for his son Joffrey. Following Robert’s death, Eddard loses the political war against the Lannisters and is publicly decapitated.
